### Explanation of Postconditions:

1. **totalSupply**: The postcondition ensures that the returned supply matches the total supply stored in the contract.
  
2. **transfer**: The postconditions cover three scenarios:
   - A successful transfer decreases the sender's balance by `_value`.
   - A self-transfer does not change the sender's balance.
   - If the transfer fails, the balance remains unchanged.

3. **transferFrom**: Similar to the `transfer` function, it checks:
   - A successful transfer decreases both the sender's and the allowance for the spender.
   - A self-transfer does not change the sender's balance.
   - If the transfer fails, the balances and the allowance remain unchanged.

4. **approve**: The postcondition checks that on a successful call, the allowance is set to the new value. If it fails, the allowance remains unchanged.

5. **balanceOf**: The postcondition ensures that the returned balance matches the value stored in `_balances`.

6. **allowance**: The postcondition ensures that the returned remaining allowance matches the value stored in `_allowed`. 

These postconditions are consistent with the ERC20 standard and ensure that the contract behaves as expected.
